About Me

I am a Research Associate in Quantitative Social Science at the University of Sheffield’s Methods Institute.

My research explores how spatial inequalities create cascading disadvantages across education, transport, and health. I focus on computational and statistical methods — especially MAIHDA (Multilevel Analysis of Individual Heterogeneity and Discriminatory Accuracy) — to study intersectional inequalities.

Research Interests: Spatial Inequalities · Computational Social Science · Health Geography · Intersectionality · Quantitative Methods

Research Areas

Educational Segregation

20-year analysis of ethnic school segregation patterns in England using multi-group, multi-scale decomposition methods.

Spatial Accessibility

Examining how transport infrastructure shapes educational equity and social mobility using agent-based modelling and accessibility mapping.

Computational Health Geography

Developing MAIHDA extensions — Causal-MAIHDA, Spatial-MAIHDA, ML-MAIHDA — to understand how place, identity, and health intersect.
